Dogs info

The dog is a domesticated descendant of the wolf. Also called the domestic dog, it was domesticated from an extinct population of Pleistocene wolves over 14,000 years ago. The dog was the first species to be domesticated by humans.

Dogs


Dogs are the most variable mammal on Earth, with artificial selection producing upward of 360 globally recognized breeds.

    The history of Dogs.

    • In the Beginning | The Miacis

      60 million years ago

      Miacis is an extinct genus of carnivorous mammals that lived during the late Paleocene to the early Eocene epochs, roughly 65 to 33 million years ago. Often considered a basal member of the order Carnivora, Miacis represents an early form of carnivores that eventually gave rise to the modern groups of cats, dogs, and other related species. Its appearance was similar to that of a small, weasel-like animal, and it likely occupied a niche similar to that of modern-day small predators.

    • 40-30 million years ago

      The Cynodictis

      Cynodictis is another early carnivorous mammal from the Eocene epoch, living around 40 to 30 million years ago. It is considered a part of the early evolution of the Carnivora order and is thought to be related to the ancestors of modern canids (dog-like mammals).

    • 15 million years ago

      The Tomarctus

      Tomarctus is an extinct genus of early canid from the Miocene epoch, approximately 20 to 10 million years ago. It represents an early branch of the canid family, which includes modern dogs, wolves, foxes, and other relatives.

    • 1 million years ago

      Wolf

      Wolves are large, social carnivores belonging to the genus Canis. The most well-known species is the gray wolf (Canis lupus), which is found in various parts of the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

    • Domestication of Dogs

      Dogs are descendants of wolves, and the process of domestication probably began between 20,000 and 40,000 years ago, though exact dates are still debated. Early humans and wolves likely formed a mutually beneficial relationship. Wolves scavenged near human campsites, while humans gained a warning system and assistance with hunting from these proto-dogs.
